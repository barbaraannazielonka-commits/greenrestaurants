📖 Reading — Green Restaurants

💡 Read the text carefully. Hover over yellow wordsThis is a definition! to see their meaning. Then answer the questions in Task 4.
Changing Habits

In order to achieve a Green Restaurant CertificateAn official document showing a restaurant is eco-friendly, the owners must make changes in many areas. First, water efficiencyUsing water carefully so you don't waste any is important. By installing low flush toiletsToilets that use less water per flush, dishwashers that use less water, and taps with a low flow rate, restaurants can save thousands of litres of water. Second, waste reductionUsing less materials and throwing away less rubbish is also of great importance. In the USA, for example, an average restaurant can produce 150,000 pounds (70,000 kg) of rubbish each year. By recyclingProcessing used materials to make new products, reusing or compostingTurning food waste into fertiliser for soil rubbish and leftovers, restaurants will be able to reduce their waste considerably. Finally, a third focus area is reducing water pollutionWhen harmful substances get into water. Chemical waste can be avoided if soap and cleaning products are replaced with eco-friendly alternativesProducts that don't damage the environment.
Raw Materials

Going green is also about what is on the menuThe list of food and drink in a restaurant. Eco-friendly restaurants serve organicFood grown without chemicals or artificial fertilisers and sustainable food. Focus areas here are meat production, fishery depletionWhen too many fish are caught and fish numbers go down, use of pesticidesChemicals used to kill insects that damage plants in agriculture, and transport of foodMoving food from where it is grown to the restaurant. For example, meat should be animal welfare approvedMeans animals are treated well and not given hormones. This means that no hormones or antibiotics have been used on the animals, and that chicken and eggs are cage freeAnimals are not kept in small cages. Finally, it is estimated that the average food product travels 1,500 miles to get from farm to plate. Choosing locally-grown foodFood produced near the restaurant, not transported far means less pollution and less energy used for transport and refrigeration during transport.
Energy

Another important criterionA standard or rule used for making a judgement for certification of green restaurants is to reduce the use of energy. Most hotels and restaurants use a lot of electricity for heating, cooling, ventilationThe system that brings fresh air into a building, water heating and lighting. Choosing clean sources of energyEnergy from wind, sun or water — not oil or gas where possible and installing more energy-efficient equipmentMachines that do the same job using less electricity are both important steps towards a green restaurant. Occupancy sensorsDevices that turn lights off when no one is in the room in toilets and offices, daylight sensors in dining rooms, LED lampsLight bulbs that use much less electricity than old ones, and convection ovensOvens that use a fan to cook food faster and use less energy are examples of smart investments. The basic rules are quite simply: "shut it off, turn it down and keep it clean."
